PARAMETER SPACE EXPLORATION - INTERPRETATION GUIDE
===================================================

This figure shows how different parameter combinations affect the LD/DD 
amplitude ratio. Each panel is a 2D "slice" through the multi-dimensional
parameter space.

COLOR CODING:
=============
Blue regions:   LD < DD (ratio < 1.0) - Masking dominates
White region:   LD ≈ DD (ratio ≈ 1.0) - Balanced
Red regions:    LD > DD (ratio > 1.0) - Entrainment dominates

Black contour lines mark:
- Dashed line at 0.8 (LD is 80% of DD)
- Solid line at 1.0 (LD = DD)
- Dashed line at 1.2 (LD is 120% of DD)

SPECIES MARKERS:
===============
● Red circle (Larinioides):   In blue region (LD < DD)
■ Blue square (Agelenopsis):  Near white line (LD ≈ DD)
▲ Orange triangle (Steatoda): In red region (LD > DD)

===================================================
PANEL-BY-PANEL INTERPRETATION:
===================================================

PANEL A: Light Sensitivity vs Masking Strength
-----------------------------------------------
★ MOST IMPORTANT PANEL ★

This shows the two competing mechanisms:
• X-axis (Light Sensitivity): Entrainment - INCREASES LD amplitude
• Y-axis (Masking Strength): Suppression - DECREASES LD amplitude

Key observations:
1. DIAGONAL PATTERN: 
   - Top-left (high masking, low entrainment) → Blue (LD < DD)
   - Bottom-right (low masking, high entrainment) → Red (LD > DD)
   - Diagonal band → White (LD ≈ DD)

2. SPECIES SEPARATION:
   - Larinioides (●): Top-left corner
     High masking (0.9) + Low entrainment (0.09) → Strong blue
   
   - Agelenopsis (■): Near diagonal, bottom-left
     Low masking (0.1) + Very low entrainment (0.06) → Near white
   
   - Steatoda (▲): Bottom-right area
     Weak masking (0.25) + High entrainment (0.75) → Red

3. INTERPRETATION:
   This panel clearly shows the three species occupy DISTINCT
   regions of parameter space, representing three different
   strategies for balancing entrainment and masking.

PANEL B: Light Sensitivity vs Baseline Activity
-----------------------------------------------
Shows how circadian entrainment interacts with opportunism.

Key observations:
1. HORIZONTAL GRADIENT (light sensitivity dominates):
   - Left side (low sensitivity) → Blue
   - Right side (high sensitivity) → Red
   
2. VERTICAL VARIATION (baseline has modest effect):
   - Higher baseline → Slightly reduces contrast
   - But doesn't change blue/red pattern

3. SPECIES POSITIONS:
   - Larinioides (●): Low baseline, low sensitivity → Blue
   - Agelenopsis (■): High baseline, low sensitivity → Blue/white
   - Steatoda (▲): Mid baseline, high sensitivity → Red

4. INTERPRETATION:
   Light sensitivity is the dominant factor here. Baseline
   activity modulates the effect but doesn't reverse it.
   Species differ primarily in light sensitivity.

PANEL C: Masking Strength vs Baseline Activity
----------------------------------------------
Shows how light suppression interacts with opportunism.

Key observations:
1. VERTICAL GRADIENT (masking dominates):
   - Top (high masking) → Blue
   - Bottom (low masking) → Red/white
   
2. HORIZONTAL VARIATION (baseline has modest effect):
   - Higher baseline → Slight reduction in blue intensity
   - But pattern stays similar

3. SPECIES POSITIONS:
   - Larinioides (●): No baseline, high masking → Strong blue
   - Agelenopsis (■): High baseline, low masking → White
   - Steatoda (▲): Mid baseline, mid masking → Orange/white

4. INTERPRETATION:
   Masking strength is the primary determinant. Baseline
   activity has a secondary modulatory role. The three
   species span the range from strong masking (Larinioides)
   to weak masking (Agelenopsis/Steatoda).

PANEL D: Light Sensitivity vs Gate Recovery (tau_h)
--------------------------------------------------
Shows how entrainment interacts with peak width.

Key observations:
1. HORIZONTAL GRADIENT (light sensitivity dominates):
   - Similar to Panel B
   - Left → Blue, Right → Red
   
2. VERTICAL VARIATION (tau_h has minimal effect):
   - Peak width doesn't strongly affect LD/DD ratio
   - Slight variation but overall pattern preserved

3. SPECIES POSITIONS:
   - Larinioides (●): Low sensitivity, fast gate (tau_h=2)
   - Agelenopsis (■): Very low sensitivity, slow gate (tau_h=3)
   - Steatoda (▲): High sensitivity, slow gate (tau_h=3)

4. INTERPRETATION:
   Peak width (tau_h) has minimal impact on LD/DD ratio.
   Light sensitivity is the dominant factor. This suggests
   peak width affects peak SHAPE more than LD/DD ratio.

===================================================
KEY INSIGHTS FROM PARAMETER SPACE EXPLORATION:
===================================================

1. TWO PATHWAYS DETERMINE LD/DD RATIO:
   Panel A shows that light_sensitivity and masking_strength
   are the PRIMARY determinants. These create opposing forces:
   - Entrainment (light_sensitivity) → LD > DD
   - Masking (masking_strength) → LD < DD

2. BASELINE ACTIVITY IS SECONDARY:
   Panels B and C show baseline_activity modulates but doesn't
   reverse the effects of entrainment and masking.

3. PEAK WIDTH IS TERTIARY:
   Panel D shows tau_h has minimal effect on LD/DD ratio,
   suggesting it primarily affects peak shape rather than
   the balance between LD and DD amplitudes.

4. SPECIES OCCUPY DISTINCT REGIONS:
   The three species are well-separated in parameter space,
   particularly in Panel A (the most important panel). This
   suggests:
   - Larinioides: "Strong masking" strategy
   - Agelenopsis: "Balanced" strategy  
   - Steatoda: "Strong entrainment" strategy

5. CONTINUUM, NOT DISCRETE TYPES:
   The smooth color gradients show that LD/DD ratio varies
   continuously across parameter space. The three species
   represent points on a continuum rather than discrete
   categories.

6. PREDICTIVE POWER:
   Given any new species' parameters, we can predict its
   LD/DD ratio by finding its position on these heatmaps.
   Panel A provides the most accurate prediction.
